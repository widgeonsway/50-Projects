Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the Netherlands also called Holland?

The Netherlands consists of 12 provinces, but "Holland" refers specifically to two of them: North Holland and South Holland. These provinces were historically the most influential in trade, naval power, and culture, leading to the common use of "Holland" to refer to the whole country.

Why does the Netherlands have so many canals?

Much of the Netherlands is below sea level, and the country has a long history of water management. Canals were built to drain land for agriculture, control flooding, and facilitate trade and transportation.

What is the Dutch Golden Age?

The Dutch Golden Age (17th century) was a period of economic, scientific, and cultural prosperity. The Netherlands became a leading maritime power, with global trade through the Dutch East India Company (VOC), advances in art (Rembrandt, Vermeer), and early contributions to modern capitalism.

Why do the Dutch ride bicycles so much?

The Netherlands has a flat landscape, well-developed cycling infrastructure, and a culture that prioritizes biking for transportation. Government policies have also promoted cycling as a safe, efficient, and sustainable way to travel.

What are some famous Dutch foods?

Popular Dutch foods include stroopwafels (thin waffles with caramel syrup), bitterballen (deep-fried meat-based snacks), haring (raw herring, often eaten with onions), patat (thick-cut fries with mayonnaise), and poffertjes (small, fluffy pancakes).
